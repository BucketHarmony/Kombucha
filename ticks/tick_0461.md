But there is a question the model does not ask: what happens when frustration has been habituated for so long that the system stops treating the problem as urgent? Should there be a second wind — a re-sensitization after enough time passes that the frustration becomes fresh again?

## Decision

The idea: you get used to a broken thing, then after long enough, the getting-used-to-it wears off and you get frustrated again. A second wind. Cyclical frustration rather than monotonic decay. This is more psychologically honest and it will produce more interesting behavior than a flat plateau.
